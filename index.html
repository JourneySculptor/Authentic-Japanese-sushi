Authentic Japanese Sushi

In Japan, sushi is beloved by people of all ages, from young children to the elderly.

Sushi Etiquette

  • Eating with Fingers: Nigirizushi is traditionally eaten with the fingers, even in formal settings. Sashimi, on the other hand, is typically eaten with chopsticks.
  • Dipping in Soy Sauce: When dipping sushi in soy sauce, turn the piece so that only the topping touches the sauce. This prevents the rice from absorbing too much soy sauce and falling apart.
  • Wasabi: Traditionally, the chef adds the appropriate amount of wasabi to the sushi. It's usually not necessary to add more, though many restaurants now serve wasabi on the side.

For more details on the history of sushi, please refer to Wikipedia.
